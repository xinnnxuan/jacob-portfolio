The Story Behind the Grind

Marketing, Mountains, and the Pursuit of Excellence

Why I Love What I Do

My journey into marketing wasn't planned—it was discovered through a love for connecting people with experiences that matter. Growing up in Spokane, I learned early that the best stories aren't just told; they're lived. Whether I'm crafting a campaign for Spokane Racquet Club or training for my next Ironman, I'm driven by the same principle: authentic connection creates lasting impact.

Marketing, to me, is about understanding the human experience. When I work with brands like Wilson and Luxilon, I'm not just selling products—I'm helping athletes find the tools that will push them beyond their limits. It's the same mindset I bring to my own training: every detail matters, every decision counts.

What Drives Me as an Endurance Athlete

Endurance sports taught me that limits are just suggestions. Every 5 AM swim session, every 100-mile bike ride, every marathon finish line—they all taught me that the impossible becomes possible when you break it down into manageable steps. This philosophy drives everything I do, from building marketing campaigns to coaching tennis players.
